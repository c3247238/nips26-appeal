# Ideation Critique

## The Good Pivot

- `cand_diag` is the correct front-runner.
- The workspace already falsifies a TIGER-centered method paper and supports a narrower diagnostic/evaluation paper instead.
- The best surviving idea is not “we found the best controller”; it is “the field is comparing revision methods too loosely.”

## Where the Idea Still Overpromises

- The idea promises a phase diagram and a mechanism answer, but the current evidence is still aggregate-level.
- H3 is still too ambitious in its present wording: the workspace shows that calibration is a useful observer, not that the three signals already explain revision benefit in the stronger sense proposed.
- The failure-taxonomy idea is currently narrower than advertised; the only explicit taxonomy asset is the HumanEval syntax/runtime split.

## Strategic Reading of the Current Evidence

- Honest compute is real, but currently narrow: it supports a correction to how comparisons are reported, not a wholesale rewrite of the ranking story.
- Observer vs. controller is a strong framing idea, but it still needs bucket-level evidence to feel like a discovery rather than a good interpretation.
- Code is valuable as a boundary condition, not as generalization support.

## Decision Rule for the Next Iteration

- Stay with `cand_diag` only if a bucket audit reveals structured harmed/fixed/no-effect regions that make the mechanism story tangible.
- Narrow the paper to an evaluation-correction study if the bucket audit stays noisy.
- If the bucket audit shows dependency-sensitive failure clusters, `cand_factorization` becomes a cleaner next research move than continuing to tune controllers.
- Do not reopen new controller search until buckets, uncertainty, and asset discipline are fixed.

## Bottom Line

- The idea is now strategically correct.
- It is not yet evidentially complete enough to justify the strongest mechanism-language now appearing in the paper draft.
