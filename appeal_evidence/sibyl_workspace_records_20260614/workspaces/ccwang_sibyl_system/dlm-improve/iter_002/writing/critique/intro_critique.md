# Section Critique: Introduction

**Score**: 8/10

## Summary

The Introduction has the right paper identity. It clearly frames the manuscript as a compute-normalized diagnostic/protocol paper and avoids falling back into a new-controller story.

## Main issues

1. The opening still moves quickly from framing into evidence. A slightly cleaner separation between motivation and result preview would improve readability.
2. The section should eventually carry citation anchors for LLaDA, Dream, and the broader training-free revision literature.
3. The teaser figure reference would be stronger if the prose explicitly mapped each panel to one headline claim.

## Recommended revision

- Keep one anchor fact per claim and move the rest of the numeric detail into Section 4.
- Add citation placeholders near the first mention of model families and prior method categories.
- Add one sentence explaining the three teaser panels.
