# Claim Boundary

## Supported by Current Evidence

1. `cand_espd` is the only iteration-4 line with direct full-scale support.
2. Under the current GSM8K runtime contract, `cand_espd` shows a modest but credible quality-speed trade-off improvement relative to `ESPD-FixedFrontier`.
3. Entropy is better supported as a routing/stopping signal than as a semantic controller.

## Not Supported by Current Evidence

1. Benchmark-level SOTA or broad superiority claims.
2. A strong statistical claim that `cand_espd` decisively beats all controls.
3. Validation of the object-level mainline (`cand_bsr`).
4. Generalization beyond the current full-scale GSM8K setting.
5. A cleanly isolated proof that the candidate-sham gap is purely mechanistic rather than partially implementation-mediated.

## Hypothesis Status

- `H1 Local Repair Object`: inconclusive
- `H2 Entropy-As-Routing`: supported modestly
- `H3 Benefit-Gating`: untested
- `H4 Runtime-Parity Robustness`: partially supported
- `H5 Structured-Code Robustness`: untested
