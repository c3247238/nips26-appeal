# Introduction Critique

**Score: 7.6/10**

## Strengths

- The section establishes a clear bounded claim and avoids overselling.
- The motivation is timely: small-gain dLLM research needs attribution discipline.
- The contribution list is aligned with the actual available artifacts.

## Main Issues

1. The introduction names several neighboring directions but does not yet anchor them with explicit citations.
2. The contribution list could distinguish more sharply between empirical contribution and evaluation/attribution contribution.
3. The final paragraph previews sections well, but it does not preview the paper's strongest empirical contrast (`cand_espd` vs `ESPD-FixedFrontier`) prominently enough.

## Suggested Revisions

- Add explicit citation placeholders for the neighboring dLLM test-time scaling and calibration lines.
- Tighten the contribution bullets into three crisp, reviewer-facing claims.
- Mention the candidate-versus-sham result once more at the end of the introduction as the paper's central empirical hook.
