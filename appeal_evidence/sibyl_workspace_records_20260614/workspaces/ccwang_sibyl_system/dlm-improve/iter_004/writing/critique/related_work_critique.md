## Main Issues

1. The section is under-cited in its current draft form and reads more like a narrative summary than a publication-ready related work section.
2. The discussion of specific neighboring work remains generic; the reader still needs sharper distinctions between our setting and lines such as search-heavy, confidence-guided, or memory-augmented dLLMs.
3. The calibration/failure-prediction paragraph is conceptually strong but could connect more explicitly to the candidate/sham comparison used later in the paper.

## Suggested Revisions

- Add explicit citation anchors for the foundational dLLM papers and the 2025-2026 neighboring work listed in `context/literature.md`.
- Add one sentence per subgroup clarifying why each line is not the same as the present training-free routing setup.
- End the section with a short positioning paragraph that states the exact novelty boundary of the paper.
