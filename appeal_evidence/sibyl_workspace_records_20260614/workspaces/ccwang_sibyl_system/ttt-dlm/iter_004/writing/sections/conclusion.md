# 6. Conclusion

We have introduced two training-free inference-time scaling methods for masked diffusion language models that directly address the information island problem --- the systematic loss of distributional information between denoising steps. Belief-State Diffusion (BSD) replaces hard mask embeddings with continuously evolving distributional representations that accumulate cross-step information via EMA updates, while Adaptive Classifier-Free Guidance (A-CFG) amplifies reasoning signals through confidence-based re-masking and dual forward passes. Evaluated on Dream-7B, both methods individually outperform vanilla denoising and all remasking baselines (ReMDM-conf 4.4%, RCR 5.7%) on Countdown, with A-CFG achieving the strongest pilot-scale result (12.5%) alongside DMI.

**Information-theoretic validation of continuous belief evolution.** BSD provides the first empirical evidence that continuous belief states in MDLMs converge in an information-theoretically principled manner: belief entropy decreases near-monotonically during denoising (Spearman $\rho = -0.95$, 15/16 samples monotonic), terminal belief entropy is lower than vanilla (0.001 vs 0.002), and entropy convergence quality strongly predicts task correctness ($r = 0.78$, $p < 0.001$). These results validate the hypothesis that replacing discrete mask embeddings with soft distributional representations enables meaningful information accumulation across denoising steps.

**A-CFG as a general-purpose reasoning enhancement.** A-CFG is the only method in our evaluation that demonstrates consistent cross-benchmark generalization, improving accuracy from Countdown (+12.5pp over vanilla at pilot scale) to GSM8K (+12.5pp, 37.5% vs 25.0%). This establishes classifier-free guidance --- applied via MDLMs' natural masking mechanism for unconditional input construction --- as the most promising general tool for MDLM reasoning enhancement. Notably, fixed guidance weight ($w = 1.5$) dominates all temporal scheduling variants (linear, cosine, threshold), indicating that theoretical predictions from continuous diffusion \citep{rojas2025cfg} do not transfer directly to masked diffusion's discrete dynamics.

**DMI as a practical near-zero-cost contribution.** Diffusion Memory Injection remains the most efficient training-free method discovered across four iterations of experimentation: on the full-scale Countdown-500 evaluation (3 seeds), DMI achieves 9.3\% accuracy versus vanilla 4.7\% --- approximately $2\times$ improvement --- with near-zero computational overhead ($\sim$1.05$\times$). DMI requires no backward pass, no architectural changes, and no hyperparameter tuning, making it immediately deployable as a default enhancement in any MDLM inference pipeline.

**Systematic negative results as design space constraints.** Extensive experiments reveal that several theoretically motivated approaches fail on Dream-7B, providing actionable constraints for future work: (1) cross-step JSD stability signals are degenerate (stability $\sim$0.997 everywhere), rendering RACFG-style re-masking ineffective (0\% accuracy); (2) CFG temporal scheduling does not transfer from continuous to masked diffusion (all scheduled variants yield 0\% vs fixed guidance at 12.5\%); (3) BSD and A-CFG are non-composable --- their combination (6.2\%) underperforms both individual methods, as BSD's smooth belief distributions optimized for refinement conflict with A-CFG's requirement for sharp, discriminable distributions; and (4) parameter-space adaptation (DTA) produces insufficient gradient signal in MDLMs (MLM loss at 0.005--0.032). These failures, together with the positive results, systematically map what does and does not work for MDLM inference-time scaling.

**Limitations.** The primary limitation of this study is statistical power: BSD and A-CFG results on Countdown and GSM8K are at pilot scale ($n = 16$), where bootstrap confidence intervals are wide and include zero. Full-scale 3-seed validation (500 samples per seed) is required for publication-ready conclusions. The documented pilot-to-full-scale effect size shrinkage across prior iterations (up to 24pp) warrants caution in interpreting the pilot-scale improvements. Additionally, compute-fair analysis suggests that vanilla denoising with proportionally more steps is competitive at matched FLOPs, indicating that the methods' advantage is concentrated at the low-compute frontier rather than offering universal dominance.

**Future directions.** Three avenues emerge from our findings. First, *training-aware belief states* --- learning belief update rules via a lightweight auxiliary module (cf. MetaState's GRU \citep{xia2026metastate}) --- could address BSD's sensitivity to the $k$-parameter and improve robustness beyond hand-designed EMA schedules. Second, *confidence-calibrated guidance* with learned weight schedules could overcome the failure of theory-driven temporal scheduling by adapting to the specific information geometry of masked diffusion. Third, *scaling to larger models* may provide richer cross-step distributional variation, potentially rehabilitating JSD-based stability signals that are degenerate on Dream-7B but could be informative on models with less stable denoising dynamics.
