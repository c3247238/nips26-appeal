# 6 Conclusion

Three empirical contributions transform feature absorption from a narrowly validated curiosity into a rigorously characterized phenomenon with actionable implications for SAE design.

**Absorption survives the L0 confound --- and the link strengthens.**
Across 48 Gemma Scope SAEs, 3 of 4 downstream quality metrics retain meaningful partial correlations with absorption after controlling for $L_0$, width, and layer. The sparse probing result is the most striking: the partial correlation *strengthened* from $r = -0.664$ to $r = -0.746$ ($p = 1.2 \times 10^{-9}$) when $L_0$ was added as a covariate, revealing a classical suppression effect in which $L_0$ had been masking absorption's true association with quality. Baron-Kenny mediation analysis establishes absorption as the primary pathway through which $L_0$ affects SCR (full mediation; direct effect $c' = -0.003$, n.s.; indirect effect $= 0.025$, 95% CI $[0.007, 0.048]$) and TPP (full mediation; proportion mediated $= 0.54$). Rosenbaum sensitivity analysis under Mahalanobis matching (17 pairs) yields $\Gamma = 2.65$ for TPP, indicating the result can withstand a 2.65:1 odds ratio from an unmeasured confounder before losing significance. The within-width matching null --- no significant quality differences between high- and low-absorption SAEs matched on width --- is an important caveat: the causal chain is best supported in cross-width comparisons, and within-width evidence requires larger samples than the current $n = 15$--$18$ per stratum.

**The dominance-based absorption metric does not transfer to knowledge hierarchies.**
Applying the Chanin et al. (2024) absorption metric to city--country, city--continent, and city--language hierarchies on GPT-2 Small produces dominance-based absorption rates of 51--85%. The shuffled-hierarchy control reveals the metric's failure: randomized city--attribute mappings yield 100% absorption, indistinguishable from real hierarchies. A cosine-calibrated variant that requires alignment between the dominant SAE latent's decoder direction and the probe direction detects 0% absorption across all thresholds. The root cause is GPT-2 Small's 24k SAE, with 98% dead features on city prompts: the surviving features include polysemantic "super-absorbers" (e.g., Feature 8213) that dominate at all probe-miss positions regardless of attribute type. This result is itself a methodological contribution: future cross-domain absorption studies must use cosine-calibrated metrics or larger models where knowledge features are linearly separable in the SAE decoder space.

**The 420-SAE absorption scaling surface reveals significant width--$L_0$ interaction.**
A generalized additive model fit to 420 SAEs from SAEBench (Gemma 2 2B, widths 2,304--1,048,576, $L_0$ 9.3--8,277) finds a highly significant tensor interaction term ($p = 3.1 \times 10^{-15}$), with the interaction GAM achieving $R^2 = 0.693$ versus $R^2 = 0.620$ for the additive model and $R^2 = 0.488$ for the linear model. Gradient analysis identifies a transition zone at $\log_2(L_0) \in [2.7, 3.8]$ ($L_0 \approx 7$--$14$) where absorption rises sharply. At $L_0 > 14$, absorption is low regardless of width. At $L_0 < 7$, absorption increases dramatically from 16k to 1M width. This interaction has a direct practical implication: scaling dictionary width --- the standard strategy for improving SAE quality --- amplifies absorption risk unless $L_0$ is simultaneously increased above the transition threshold.

**Methodological precedent.**
Epidemiological causal inference methods --- partial correlation with confound control, Baron-Kenny mediation, Rosenbaum sensitivity bounds, Bradford Hill criteria assessment (3 strong, 5 moderate, 0 weak) --- provide rigorous tools for establishing causal claims from observational SAE data. The SAE evaluation literature currently relies on uncorrected correlations between architectures that differ simultaneously in width, $L_0$, layer, and training procedure. These methods, standard in medical and social science research, directly address this confounding problem and are applicable to any observational comparison between SAE families.

**Limitations and future work.**
The confound analysis is limited to 48 SAEs from Gemma 2 2B; cross-architecture replication on Llama, Qwen, and Mistral SAEs is needed to establish generality. The cross-domain experiment uses GPT-2 Small due to Gemma 2B access restrictions; repeating on larger models with dedicated knowledge features will determine whether absorption genuinely does not occur in knowledge hierarchies or whether the GPT-2 Small SAE simply lacks the capacity to reveal it. The mediation analysis assumes a correctly specified causal ordering ($L_0 \to$ absorption $\to$ quality); reverse causation or common causes cannot be ruled out with the current cross-sectional design. The taxonomy correction confirms the original 92.3% combined absorption rate on GPT-2 Small (0 of 26 letters changed classification under frequency-matched comparison tokens), establishing that the high rate reflects genuine feature selectivity rather than measurement artifact. The most pressing follow-up is an interventional experiment: deliberately reducing absorption (e.g., via OrtSAE or Matryoshka training) and measuring whether downstream quality improves, which would convert the current correlational evidence into causal evidence.
