## Consistency Issues Found and Fixed

1. **Table renumbering:** Section drafts used "Table 5" for probe degradation and "Table 4" for hypothesis verdicts. The integrated paper uses Tables 1--5 sequentially (probes, cross-domain rates, degradation, hedging, hypotheses).
2. **Title change:** The original outline title "The Absorption Tax: How Feature Hierarchy Structure Governs SAE Failure Modes Across Semantic Domains" was changed to "Cross-Domain Feature Absorption in Sparse Autoencoders: Measurement, Mechanism, and Probe Quality Confounds" to avoid leading with the failed theoretical framework (evolution lesson: TITLE FEATURES A FAILED THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK).
3. **Section 5 and 6 construction:** These sections were not present as standalone drafts. Content was synthesized from the outline specification, discussion back-references, and consolidation_summary.json data. All quantitative claims are traced to source data files.
4. **Structural redundancy reduction:** The control failure finding (absorption below shuffled controls) is stated once in Section 3.3 (controls) rather than repeated across multiple sections (evolution lesson: STRUCTURAL REDUNDANCY).
5. **Overclaiming mitigation:** Theoretical claims downgraded per evolution lessons. Section 5.3 uses "decoder information entanglement" with circularity caveat consistently. CMI is described as having p=0.059/0.236--level evidence, not as "predicting" absorption. The Absorption Tax is described as "not supported" rather than featured in the title.
6. **Figure 1 left panel:** The mechanism schematic (fig1_mechanism_desc.md) describes a TikZ diagram for LaTeX rendering. The current PDF (fig1_teaser.pdf) is the bar chart only. The schematic will need to be composed alongside the bar chart during LaTeX typesetting. This is noted but not a blocker for the markdown integration.
