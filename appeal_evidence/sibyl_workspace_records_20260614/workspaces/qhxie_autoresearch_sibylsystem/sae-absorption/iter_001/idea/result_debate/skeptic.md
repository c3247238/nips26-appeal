# Skeptic Analysis: Feature Absorption in SAEs (Post-R4 Update)

**Role:** Skeptical statistician  
**Date:** 2026-04-13  
**Data basis:** R3 full experiments + R4 blocking experiments (direct labels, shuffled controls, Llama replication)  
**Verdict:** The R4 experiments resolved several prior ambiguities — mostly by falsifying claims. The remaining paper has two defensible contributions, one of which (EDA detection) carries a novelty problem that was not present in R3. The data supports a narrow, honest characterization paper, not the ambitious multi-contribution study originally proposed.

---

## 1. Statistical Risk Inventory (Top 3)

### Risk 1: EDA = 1 - DecCos (mathematical identity), destroying independent novelty of EDA as a metric

R4 confirms across all three model families (Gemma 2B, GPT-2 Small, Llama-3.1-8B) that EDA and negated decoder-encoder cosine similarity have Pearson r = -1.0. The writing gate itself states: "EDA = 1 - dec_cosine (mathematically equivalent to existing SAEBench metric)." This is not a minor equivalence — it means the paper's primary detection metric is a trivially re-parameterized version of an existing metric already computed by SAEBench. The writing gate acknowledges this limitation. The theoretical "lower bound theorem" thus provides a theoretical justification for an existing metric, not a novel metric. This collapses the claimed distinction between "our new metric" and "prior work" to zero at the formula level.

**Specific evidence:** `r4a_eda_direct_validation.json` lines showing `delta_eda_vs_deccosine: 0.0` for both GPT-2 configs; `r4c_llama_replication.json` showing `pearson_r: -0.9999999403953552` for Llama. Every architecture confirms the identity.

### Risk 2: H3 cross-domain contribution is now FALSIFIED, not merely "pending"

The R4 shuffled control is devastating. For all 9 hierarchy-SAE combinations, real absorption rates do NOT exceed the 95th percentile of shuffled null distributions. The ratio of real/shuffled ranges from 0.89 to 1.43, and NO combination exceeds the p95 threshold. The decision gate explicitly states: `h3_validated: false`, `n_domains_exceed_shuffled_p95: 0`, `go_no_go: "NO_GO"`. The intra-RAVEL coherence (rho = 0.924 from R3) is now revealed to be meaningless: the R4 shuffled controls also produce perfect intra-RAVEL rho = 1.0 across all hierarchy pairs. Coherence was never diagnostic — shuffled labels produce identical coherence patterns because the coherence reflects SAE-config-level variation, not hierarchy-specific absorption.

**Specific evidence:** `r4b_shuffled_control.json` shows `shuffled_intra_ravel_spearman_rho` = 1.0 for all three pairings, identical to real data. The "existence proof" that was "not contested" in the R3 synthesis is now empirically falsified.

### Risk 3: The "3/8 configs pass" EDA framing relies on mixing proxy and direct labels at different stringency levels

The writing gate claims 3 passing configs: Gemma L5-16k (0.698 proxy), Gemma L12-16k (0.776 proxy), GPT-2 L6 (0.650 direct). But these use different label sources (Neuronpedia proxy vs. FeatureAbsorptionCalculator direct), different models, and different SAE widths. The best direct-label AUROC (GPT-2 L6 = 0.650) barely clears the 0.65 threshold, with a 95% CI of [0.531, 0.761] — the lower bound is below chance-level for a test of whether EDA exceeds random. The two Gemma "passing" configs use proxy labels that R4 could not validate (because Gemma 2B remains gated). We do not know whether these proxy-label AUROCs would hold up under direct labels. The pilot-to-full collapse at L12-65k (0.853 -> 0.468) demonstrates that proxy labels can inflate AUROC dramatically.

**Specific evidence:** GPT-2 L6 CI lower bound = 0.531, n_pos = 18. With only 18 positive examples, the bootstrap AUROC has boot_std = 0.059. The Gemma proxy-label results cannot be cross-validated.

## 2. Alternative Explanations

### For EDA AUROC > 0.65 on GPT-2 L6 (the only direct-label passing config):

**Alternative: Small sample size artifact.** n_pos = 18 absorbed latents out of 24,576. The absorbed-vs-non-absorbed EDA difference is 0.670 vs. 0.631 (a delta of 0.040 on a scale with std = 0.076). Cohen's d = 0.53, which is "medium" effect size, but with only 18 positives, the bootstrap distribution is wide. Furthermore, the non-absorbed sample contains latents with EDA up to 1.35 (far above any absorbed latent — max absorbed EDA is 0.75). If even 2-3 of these high-EDA non-absorbed latents were reclassified as absorbed (due to label noise or threshold effects in FeatureAbsorptionCalculator), the AUROC would likely drop below 0.65. The entire detection claim rests on the correct classification of ~18 latents.

### For Gemma L12-16k AUROC = 0.776 (strongest result, but proxy labels):

**Alternative: Neuronpedia proxy labels are a systematically biased subset.** Neuronpedia labels are generated by automated descriptions from an LLM. If the LLM systematically mislabels certain feature types (e.g., if features with high encoder-decoder misalignment happen to be described in terms that the proxy-label pipeline interprets as "absorbed"), the resulting AUROC reflects the pipeline's bias, not genuine EDA detection power. We cannot rule this out because the R4 attempt to validate with direct labels on Gemma was blocked by model access. The only model where direct-label validation was possible (GPT-2) showed a much weaker AUROC (0.650 vs. 0.776).

### For the "75% early absorption dominance" taxonomy finding:

**Alternative: The threshold (tau = 0.3) was chosen post-hoc to maximize early-type proportion.** The threshold sensitivity analysis reveals that the early-type proportion ranges from 32% (tau = 0.2) to 95% (tau = 0.4) on L12-65k. At the data-driven threshold (p95 of random cosine = 0.044), virtually ALL latents would be classified as "late" (since nearly all latents have max_cos_dk_parent_probe > 0.044). The "dominance" of early absorption at tau = 0.3 is entirely an artifact of threshold selection. No data-driven method was used to select tau = 0.3 — it was a researcher degree of freedom.

## 3. Proxy Metric Audit

### Gap 1: EDA measures encoder-decoder misalignment, which is identical to 1 - decoder cosine similarity

The original framing claimed EDA was a "novel" metric distinct from existing measures. R4 confirms EDA = 1 - cos(w_e, d), which is literally the negation of the encoder-decoder cosine similarity already computed by SAEBench. The paper now measures "what we already measure" while "claiming we measure something new." The Formal EDA Lower Bound Theorem provides a theoretical lower bound for an existing metric — this is a valid contribution at the theorem level but cannot be presented as a new detection method.

### Gap 2: The cross-domain "existence proof" (all 18 measurements > 3x random) is invalidated by R4 shuffled controls

The R3 synthesis ranked this as "the paper's most novel empirical finding." R4 shows that shuffled (random) hierarchy labels produce equivalent absorption rates. The 3x-random threshold was met equally by random labels, meaning the "existence proof" was measuring SAE structural properties (e.g., feature co-occurrence patterns in the decoder dictionary) rather than genuine hierarchical absorption. The entire cross-domain narrative collapses.

### Gap 3: "Early absorption dominance" depends on an undefined measurement of "parent probe direction"

The taxonomy classifies absorbed latents by max cos(d_k, parent_probe). But the "parent probe" in the experiments is a proxy: the mean decoder direction of absorbed latents. This is circular — we identify absorbed latents first, then compute a "parent probe" from their decoders, then use that probe to classify them into subtypes. If the parent probe is inaccurate (which it likely is, given that proper model probes were never obtained), the early/late classification is unreliable.

## 4. Severity Classification

### Fatal Flaws

**F1. Cross-domain generalization (H3) is FALSIFIED — real rates indistinguishable from shuffled null.**
This was previously the "cleanest novelty claim in the paper" (per the R3 proposal). R4 shuffled controls show 0/9 hierarchy-SAE combinations exceed the shuffled p95 threshold. The intra-RAVEL coherence (rho = 0.924) is replicated identically by shuffled labels (rho = 1.0). The entire cross-domain contribution must be withdrawn, not merely "downgraded." Any paper that retains cross-domain claims without same-model probes is making unsupported assertions contradicted by its own shuffled controls.

**F2. EDA is not a novel metric — it is 1 minus an existing SAEBench metric.**
The writing gate and R4 validation both confirm EDA = 1 - DecCos with r = -1.0. The paper cannot present "a new weight-only absorption detector" when the metric is the negation of an existing one. The Theorem is novel; the metric is not. This distinction must be front-and-center. If reviewers discover EDA = 1 - DecCos (which they will, since SAEBench is a standard benchmark), the paper's credibility collapses unless this is pre-emptively disclosed and the novelty claim repositioned to the theoretical result and the regime characterization, not the metric itself.

### Serious Concerns

**S1. The best direct-label AUROC (0.650) barely clears the threshold with n_pos = 18.**
GPT-2 L6 is the paper's only methodologically sound detection data point (direct labels from the canonical FeatureAbsorptionCalculator). Its 95% CI [0.531, 0.761] includes values consistent with chance (0.5). The effect size (Cohen's d = 0.53) is moderate but rests on 18 absorbed latents. A reviewer could reasonably argue this does not constitute "reliable detection."

**S2. The taxonomy's 75% early-dominance claim is threshold-dependent and based on 2 SAE configs.**
At tau = 0.2, early-dominance drops to 32% on L12-65k. At tau = 0.4, it rises to 95%. The "dominant" subtype changes with a single parameter choice. Furthermore, only 2 SAE configs were evaluated (L12-16k with n=16, L12-65k with n=65). L12-16k is deeply underpowered (KW p = 0.24, not significant). The entire taxonomy rests on L12-65k alone.

**S3. Llama replication is weight-only — no AUROC computed, no detection validation.**
R4C states: "Llama-3.1-8B model is HF-gated (activations inaccessible)... Absorption labels: not available." The Llama results provide only EDA distribution statistics and cross-architecture correlation (EDA = 1 - DecCos confirmed). This is not "cross-model EDA replication" — it is "cross-model confirmation that a mathematical identity holds." No detection claim can be made for Llama.

**S4. GPT-2 L10 AUROC = 0.344 (reversed direction) is unexplained and un-remediated.**
At GPT-2 L10, absorbed latents have LOWER EDA than non-absorbed (Cohen's d = -0.37, p = 0.0008, highly significant in the wrong direction). This is not merely "failure to detect" — it is active anti-prediction. If absorption can cause EDA to decrease (suggesting the encoder aligns MORE with the decoder for absorbed features), the theoretical lower bound theorem is incomplete. This edge case is buried in the data but would be devastating if highlighted by a reviewer.

### Minor Caveats

**M1. ITAC evaluated on synthetic activations only; H5 falsified.**
This is acknowledged and ITAC is already demoted. No further critique needed beyond noting that real-activation validation was never performed.

**M2. H6 scaling analysis has no residual value.**
Partial rho = +0.37 (wrong sign), R-squared = 0.18, insufficient L0 variation. The scaling "analysis" is a non-result. It should be a brief footnote, not a supplementary section.

## 5. Concrete Remediation

### For F1 (Cross-domain falsification):

**Action:** Drop all cross-domain absorption claims from primary contributions. Retain the R3 intra-RAVEL coherence (rho = 0.924) ONLY with the caveat that shuffled labels produce identical coherence (rho = 1.0), meaning the coherence reflects SAE-config-level structural variation, not hierarchy-specific signal. Report the cross-domain pipeline as a methodological contribution (the measurement framework itself is novel, even if the particular measurement failed), and the H3 falsification as an honest negative result.

**Expected outcome:** The paper loses its "first cross-domain characterization" claim but gains credibility for honest reporting. Future work with same-model probes (Gemma 2B or Llama-3.1-8B) would be the appropriate venue for cross-domain claims.

### For F2 (EDA = 1 - DecCos):

**Action:** Reframe the primary contribution from "a novel detection metric" to "a formal theoretical lower bound on an existing metric (decoder cosine similarity) that was not previously known to measure absorption, combined with the first systematic regime characterization across layers, widths, and models." The theorem (EDA >= delta^2 * sin^2(theta) / (2 + delta^2)) is genuinely novel. The empirical contribution is characterizing WHERE DecCos/EDA works (mid-layers, 16k width) and where it fails. Cite SAEBench's use of decoder cosine similarity explicitly and position the paper as providing the theoretical basis and regime map for that existing metric.

**Expected outcome:** The paper honestly frames EDA's relationship to prior work. Reviewers familiar with SAEBench will appreciate the transparency. The theoretical bound + regime characterization is a modest but genuine contribution.

### For S1 (GPT-2 L6 barely passing):

**Action:** Compute EDA AUROC on ALL available GPT-2 SAE layers (not just L6 and L10) using direct FeatureAbsorptionCalculator labels. If GPT-2 L7, L8, L9 also show AUROC > 0.65, the "mid-layer regime" claim is strengthened beyond a single data point. The direct-label methodology is available for all GPT-2 layers — there is no access constraint.

**Expected outcome:** Either multiple contiguous GPT-2 layers pass (strengthening the regime claim) or only L6 passes (weakening it to a single data point). Either result is informative.

### For S2 (Taxonomy threshold sensitivity):

**Action:** Report the full threshold sensitivity curve (fraction early vs. tau) as a primary figure, not a supplementary ablation. Identify whether any threshold shows a natural break point (e.g., bimodal distribution of max_cos_dk_parent_probe). If no natural break exists, acknowledge that the taxonomy is a continuous spectrum, not a discrete classification, and reframe the "75% early" claim as "at the canonical threshold, the majority of absorbed latents lack a corresponding decoder direction" — which is still informative for practitioners.

**Expected outcome:** The taxonomy becomes a more nuanced characterization rather than a sharp three-way partition. The practical insight (most absorption is dictionary-coverage failure) survives at any threshold where early > 50%.

### For S4 (GPT-2 L10 reversal):

**Action:** Investigate whether the FeatureAbsorptionCalculator labels at L10 identify a genuinely different type of absorption where encoder alignment is higher than non-absorbed features. Check whether L10 absorbed features are predominantly "late-type" (decoder-present, encoder-aligned but suppressed by a different mechanism). If so, the reversal is theoretically expected for late absorption and would actually support the taxonomy. If not, it remains unexplained and must be disclosed prominently.

**Expected outcome:** Either the reversal is mechanistically explained (strengthening the taxonomy) or it remains a serious limitation of EDA that must be acknowledged.

---

## Summary Verdict

**R4 results narrow the paper further than R3 suggested.** The cross-domain contribution — previously the "cleanest novelty claim" — is now empirically falsified. The EDA metric is confirmed to be mathematically identical to an existing SAEBench metric. The remaining defensible contributions are:

1. **A theoretical lower bound** on the existing decoder cosine similarity metric for absorption detection (novel theorem, not novel metric).
2. **A regime characterization** showing this metric works at mid-layers in narrow SAEs across Gemma and GPT-2 (with the caveat that the best direct-label AUROC is 0.650 with n_pos = 18).
3. **A taxonomy of absorption subtypes** showing early-type dominance (with the caveat that dominance proportion is threshold-dependent and validated on only 1 SAE config with adequate power).

This is publishable as a focused characterization paper — probably a workshop paper or short paper at EMNLP — but requires dramatically reduced claims relative to both the original proposal and the R3 synthesis. The two fatal flaws (F1 and F2) must be addressed head-on in the paper or the work will be rejected at any venue where reviewers check SAEBench or run shuffled controls.
