# 8 Conclusion

Three findings emerge from auditing the Chanin absorption metric on Gemma 2 2B JumpReLU Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) across five hierarchy domains.

**The metric does not transfer.** Shuffled-label controls exceed measured absorption rates in all five domains tested on Gemma Scope JumpReLU SAEs: first-letter (15.96% measured vs. 74.6% shuffled, 4.7$\times$), city-continent (6.49% vs. 45.2%), city-language (6.56% vs. 18.0%), animal-class (1.43% vs. 39.3%), and city-country (0.0% vs. 10.3%). At the default cosine threshold $\tau_{\cos} = 0.025$ in $\mathbb{R}^{2304}$, a random unit vector identifies 23.0% of 16,384 decoder columns as candidates, rendering the candidate identification step vacuous. Threshold sensitivity analysis across a $5 \times 4$ grid of cosine and magnitude parameters confirms this failure is structural (CV = 0.077 across 20 cells), not resolvable by threshold tuning. Confound decomposition at $L_0$=22---where all 25 $k$-sparse probes achieve F1 = 1.0---classifies 41 of 656 false negatives (6.2%) as strict hedging, where at least one of the five parent-associated latents fires at $L_0$=176, significantly above the shuffled control rate of 3.4% ($z = 3.51$, $p < 0.001$). The remaining 93.8% of false negatives resolve at higher $L_0$ through compensatory features, not parent-latent recovery. Activation patching on the 8 persistent core words---tokens that are false negatives at all four tested $L_0$ values $\{22, 41, 82, 176\}$---yields 0/8 parent recovery after child zeroing across three intervention methods (decode-reencode, residual subtraction, all-children zeroed), with 0/65 control interventions producing spurious recovery. These 8 words (eight, liked, lower, offer, often, other, under, until) are not competitively excluded; the model does not represent their first-letter information via the SAE's parent latent.

**Absorption is controlled by the $L_0$ operating point.** Absorption declines monotonically from 42.85% ($L_0$=22) to 37.49% ($L_0$=41), 14.39% ($L_0$=82), and 0.84% ($L_0$=176) on L12-16k (Spearman $\rho_s = -1.0$). Bootstrap confidence intervals at adjacent $L_0$ values do not overlap. The steepest decline falls in the $L_0 \approx 40$--$80$ range. This phase transition is stable across layers 10, 12, and 20 (CV $< 10\%$ at $L_0$=82), establishing $L_0$---a training-time hyperparameter requiring no architectural modification---as the primary control parameter for absorption severity on JumpReLU SAEs.

**The Conditional Mutual Information (CMI) diagnostic does not replicate.** At $L_0$=82 and subspace dimension $d'=10$, Spearman $\rho_s$(CMI, absorption) $= -0.383$ ($p = 0.059$, Bonferroni-corrected $p = 0.236$), with a large group-level effect (Cohen's $d = -0.924$). Partial correlation controlling for probe F1 weakens the association ($\rho_{\text{partial}} = -0.328$, $p = 0.118$); restriction to the 10 letters with F1 $> 0.85$ eliminates it ($\rho_s = -0.113$, $p = 0.757$). Replication at $L_0$=22, where all 25 probes achieve F1 = 1.0 and the probe quality confound is eliminated entirely, produces $\rho_s = +0.044$ ($p = 0.835$, Bonferroni $p = 1.0$)---the sign reverses. At higher subspace dimensions ($d' \geq 30$), the $L_0$=22 correlations become positive and nominally significant ($\rho_s = 0.410$, $p = 0.042$ at $d'=30$; $\rho_s = 0.483$, $p = 0.014$ at $d'=50$), further contradicting the rate-distortion prediction. The marginal signal at $L_0$=82 tracked probe quality, not information-theoretic structure.

Four of seven pre-registered hypotheses are falsified: hierarchy-driven dominance at $L_0$=22 (H2: predicted $>80\%$, observed 1.4%), cross-domain absorption above controls (H5), cross-domain entity matching (H6: zero matches), and architecture-specific bimodality (H7: both architectures bimodal). These negative results, reported with pre-registered targets versus observed values, constrain future work as directly as the positive findings.

Three recommendations follow for the SAE interpretability community. First, validate absorption metrics on each new SAE architecture before building mitigations: the shuffled-label control check (shuffled rate $<$ measured rate in $\geq 3$ hierarchy domains) should be standard practice. Second, report shuffled-label and random-probe controls alongside measured absorption rates in all future publications. Third, treat the $L_0$ operating point as a first-order intervention for absorption severity before pursuing encoder modifications---on JumpReLU SAEs, increasing $L_0$ from 22 to 176 reduces measured absorption by 98%.

Code and data are released as an SAEBench extension.
