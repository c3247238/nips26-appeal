# Contrarian Perspective (Iteration 2)

## Challenge to Main Direction
Fixing dead features may not fix the core problem. Even with living dictionaries, collision rates may still be near-zero correlated with downstream performance---the "absorption paradox" from iteration 1 may persist.

## Alternative Interpretation
Maybe the problem is not dead features but the concept set itself. First-letter features (a-z) may be too simple to reveal true absorption patterns. More complex hierarchies (e.g., semantic categories) may be needed.

## Critical Question
Is 1M training tokens sufficient? The dead feature ratio may be a data limitation, not a training limitation. Iteration 2 should test whether more tokens (10M) further reduces dead features.
